**Slug:** `/thoughts/when-the-agency-arrives`

**Core argument:** External agencies arrive with CEO authority and zero knowledge of existing DS architecture. The internal team either rebuilds from scratch (waste) or hacks new brand onto old architecture (debt). A strong N layer — token-first architecture plus machine-readable context — turns a rebrand into a token swap, not a rebuild.

**Why:** Lincoln lived this at Red Rooster (NCGroup). The brief was written and the agency selected before the DS team was involved. Direct experience gives this piece its authority.
