# Stakeholder Site Review — lincolnmitchell.io

## Stakeholder 1: UK Recruiter (30-second scan)

**Persona:** Sarah, senior recruiter placing DS Leads at Tier 1 London banks.

**Verdict:** Would not shortlist without a phone call first.

**What works:** /fit page, dual nationality, Breville/Fun Lab projects, "Contract or FTE."

**What's missing:** Human introduction on homepage. Downloadable CV. Years of experience. Financial services framing (healthcare IS regulated industry — frame it that way).

**What would make me say no:** The homepage. If my client opens it, they close the tab. Banks are conservative.

**One change to flip to yes:** Put a human-readable hero on the homepage — name, title, location, one sentence, "View CV" button.

## Stakeholder 2: Head of Design (5-minute review)

**Persona:** James, Head of Design at mid-size SaaS. 12-person team, budget for one senior DS hire.

**Verdict:** Would shortlist with reservations.

**What works:** /inc framework (strongest asset), "seed N so the team can scale without me," Fun Lab multi-brand, Helix career arc.

**What's missing:** Case study metrics. Team collaboration evidence. Engineer testimonials. Evidence of working within a team, not just above it.

**What would make me say no:** AI-agent framing overshadows DS expertise. /system/agents accidentally positions Lincoln as solo operator. "Not the right fit if Figma is source of truth" would alarm design team.

**One change to flip to yes:** Add team collaboration evidence to /work. Show Lincoln working with humans, not just directing agents.

## Stakeholder 3: CTO (2-minute click-through)

**Persona:** Priya, CTO at 200-person engineering-led company. Sceptical of design hires.

**Verdict:** Would interview. First "design systems" person who speaks engineering language.

**What works:** Code-first philosophy. Real tech stack. Agent governance shows system design thinking. /fit page is perfect filter.

**What's missing:** Link to actual code. Engineering concerns (testing, CI/CD, versioning). Architecture diagrams. Token pipeline visualisation.

**What would make me say no:** "One person innovation engine" positioning. /thoughts philosophical content signals essays over shipping.

**One change to flip to yes:** Technical deep-dive — real token architecture, component API, CI/CD integration, architecture diagram of token pipeline.

1. **Homepage hero with human context.** Name, title, location, years, one sentence. INC diagram stays — below a human introduction.

2. **Case study depth on /work.** Before/after. Metrics. Team collaboration evidence. Engineer quotes.

3. **Technical deep-dive.** Token architecture, component API, CI/CD, versioning. One architecture diagram. Link to real code.
