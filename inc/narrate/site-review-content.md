# Site vs Resume Content Alignment Review

## The 5 Biggest Misalignments

**1. The V7 value prop isn't on the site.**
The sharpest sentence: "Everyone at IDS showed how to make design systems readable by AI. I show how to make the thinking behind design systems readable by AI." It's in the session summary. It doesn't appear on any page — not even the homepage. This belongs above the fold.

**2. Two vocabularies for the same framework.**
The resume uses "Creative Discovery | Agentic Narrative | Product Delivery." The site uses I<N>C / Ideate / Narrate / Create. Both are correct for their audience, but a hiring manager reading the resume first and then the site will hit a terminology switch with no bridge. One line on /inc or the homepage fixes this.

**3. The career arc is absent from the site.**
CDO → React Dev → Design Systems → Agentic Design Systems is the primary proof that INC comes from lived experience, not theory. It's on the resume. It's not on the site. The /work page has the projects but doesn't narrate the sequence. A recruiter has to infer it from role titles.

**4. /work project descriptions show activities, not outcomes.**
"Led design and development of a comprehensive medical centre web application" could describe any UX lead. What actually happened at Helix: you built a NorthStar prototype, it sold the vision, you were promoted to CDO. That's the story. The site doesn't tell it.

**5. This site isn't on the work page.**
The most powerful current case study is the site itself — built by one person directing AI agents. It doesn't appear as a project on /work. The footer on /agents says "one person, six agents, zero alignment meetings" — the right sentiment, buried.

## Voice Issues

- /fit intro: "The best working relationships start with clarity about what I do — and what I don't." → Too soft. Replace with: "This page exists to save both of us time."
- /system/agents speaks in third person (Lincoln, he) while the rest of the site uses first person. Reads like a press release.
- /work descriptions use "drastically" (Fun Lab) — vague hype. Cut or replace with specific number.
