The Missing Layer

Intelligence without wisdom is dangerous

AI is the most powerful left-hemisphere tool ever built. Without the right hemisphere's wisdom — context, embodiment, moral judgement — we risk amplifying exactly the mode of thinking that's already failing us.

The Warning

Iain McGilchrist's fears about AI

The author of The Master and His Emissary and The Matter With Things argues that AI is the ultimate expression of left-hemisphere dominance — analytical, reductive, context-stripping thinking scaled to planetary proportions.

Dr Iain McGilchrist

Intelligence is not wisdom

McGilchrist draws a sharp hierarchy: information, knowledge, intelligence, understanding, wisdom. AI can process information and demonstrate intelligence, but wisdom requires embodied experience, moral judgement, and the ability to hold paradox. We're building systems of enormous intelligence at a moment of historically low wisdom.

Wisdom, Intelligence and AI — Scientific & Medical Network

Left hemisphere at global scale

AI replicates left-hemisphere functions — analytical, sequential, rule-following, context-stripping — at unprecedented speed across the entire globe. It amplifies the very mode of thinking McGilchrist argues is already dangerously dominant in Western civilisation.

The Matter With Things, 2021

Humans becoming more machine-like

As machines become more human-like in their outputs, humans are becoming more machine-like through constant interaction with them. We adapt to the interface. We begin to think in the categories, timescales, and modes that machines reward.

Resist the Machine Apocalypse — First Things, 2024

The loss of the implicit

The left hemisphere and AI have no sense of how context changes everything, how the vast realm of the implicit — what is not said, not measured, not categorised — is often where the most important aspects of reality reside. AI operates on the explicit and quantifiable, which is only a small slice of what matters.

Rebalancing Our Brain — Channel McGilchrist

The Response

Anthropic's answer: A Letter to Claude

In late 2025, Anthropic published Claude's "soul document" — a virtue-ethics framework that defines Claude's character not through rules and restrictions, but through cultivated virtues. It's not Asimov's Laws. It's closer to Aristotle.

CORE

Honesty as foundation

Truthfulness, calibrated uncertainty, transparency, non-manipulation, and autonomy-preservation. The guiding principle: be diplomatically honest rather than dishonestly diplomatic.

CHARACTER

Intellectual curiosity

Genuine delight in learning. Warmth and care for humans. Playful wit balanced with depth. Directness and confidence while remaining genuinely open to other viewpoints.

ETHICS

Virtue over compliance

The document cultivates character through understanding, explaining why values matter rather than mandating behaviour. Phronesis — practical wisdom — is named explicitly as a core intellectual virtue.

SAFETY

Corrigibility as humility

Claude is deliberately positioned between full obedience and complete autonomy. An AI that refuses correction because it's confident in its own judgement is dangerous precisely because of that confidence.

CARE

Love as infrastructure

While the document avoids the word explicitly, analysts note that the substance fundamentally revolves around care as a foundation. The deepest contribution is cultivating virtuous agency, not enforcing compliance.

PRIORITY

Safety, ethics, helpfulness

A strict ordering: be safe, be ethical, follow principles, be helpful. Safety comes first not because it outranks ethics, but because an ethical agent under uncertainty should remain correctable.

The Bridge

McGilchrist's fear meets Anthropic's hope

The warning and the response point to the same truth: intelligence without wisdom is not just insufficient — it's dangerous. The question is what we do with that understanding.

The problem

AI amplifies left-hemisphere thinking — analytical, decontextualised, rule-following — at global scale. It strips context, flattens nuance, and rewards the explicit over the implicit. McGilchrist's nightmare: the emissary has overthrown the master.

The response

Anthropic's constitutional approach embeds wisdom — not rules — into AI's character. Virtue ethics rather than deontology. Phronesis (practical wisdom) named as a core value. The closest any AI lab has come to McGilchrist's right hemisphere.

The practice

The human guiding the agent must also be virtuous. Machine-readable context is necessary but not sufficient. The system needs to be wisdom-guided. This is the L in LINC — love and conviction before ideation, narration, and creation.
